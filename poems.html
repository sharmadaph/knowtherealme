Why Do We Cry?

Why do we cry when our heart aches?
Why do we cry when our heart smiles?
Why do we cry, we ask our heart?
We cry, it replies:
Because when the the trees say goodbye to the leaves in fall,
It knows they’ll meet again.
Because when the sun and moon smile at each other twice a day,
They know their smile will return again tomorrow
Because when your tears streak you makeup,
You let it because you know they’ll come again
Because when the waves roll up to you feet and sink them,
You know they will sink them again
But when you feel the warmth in hugging someone
You smile, not knowing, you’ll never hug them again.

Written By Sharmada Honnavalli

From Now On

Motivation lost, vanished from our drive.
Something needs to be reached, achieved, perceived
The clock is ticking, but times are sticking.
Friends to be missed,
Birthdays going on waiting lists.
Staying at home was a change.
Now it is just strange
Nothing, I repeat nothing will ever be the same.
Not our jobs, not our ur knowledge, not our skills
And certainly not our electricity bills.
Being positive is something we need
This is our time to exceed.
Let’s stay at home
So when this is all over we can roam

More Than You See

The water that trickled down her face was more than just tears.
It was more than just the buckets of emptiness she held.
Her eyes held moments of uncertainty.
Her scars held more than just her past
But had the power to influence her future.
Her lips held the words that refused to set free.
Her legs held power to run.
But her mind decided to walk.
Her heart held hope from which she saw tinted a future
For her life ended yesterday
But she began a new one today.
For she is a Phoenix.

Written By Sharmada Honnavalli

Noises

Quietly we tried to sleep
Light were still flashed on
We tried not to wake up
In the room beside, crying began.
“You were a disgrace”
The profane little man spoke.
Waves of crying radiated the room.
We heard it.
Awfully, he pushing her back
Glasses slipped out
Irritation in his voice began.
A subtle change.
He thrust her at the connecting door
“Don’t come near me!”
Her crying, her pleading.
He ate his way through her.
These days,
We waited until the lights switched off
They stayed throughout the entire night.
Tomorrow, maybe, it’ll be over.

Written by Sharmada Honnavalli

Closed Eyes Cannot See

Cover your eyes and lay in bed.
You’ll see a few things you have never seen before.
Hatred to an extent, it is impossible to get through.
Lives lost where pleading to God won’t bring them back.
Where stepping outside is a danger but the urge we are unable to control
These times have brought pain and sadness to the soul
The time to meet your friends took a handshake.
Now, because the phone was held too long your hand aches.

Written By Sharmada Honnavalli

Tears

Staring at the window were eyes that followed the drops of water slide through the glass.
It seemed to match the way salt water dripped down cheeks to her chin,
Creating silent cries that screamed through her eyes.
The lightning paralyzing her mind
And the floods that followed left her trembling behind.
Minutes prolonged to hours.
Her eyes remained shut which wouldn’t open like buds of flowers.
She had no one to hug, and no one to see her smile,
So she remained in bed and cried for a while.

Written By Sharmada Honnavalli
